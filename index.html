Frequently Asked Questions

Why do you want to study electrical engineering?

I find electrical engineering incredibly fascinating because it sits at the foundation of so many modern technologies, with just how widespread electronics are. What excites me in particular though is the precision and skill involved in chip manufacturing. The fact that we are able to work at nanometer scales with so little margin for error is just jaw-dropping to me, like it's the culmination of all the technological advancements ever made. The constant drive for innovation is something that also attracts me to the field.

If you could invent anything, what would it be?

If I could invent anything, I would probably want to invent the first general artificial intelligence. I know the word “AI” is often used as a buzzword these days, but I genuinely believe that true general AI will truely transform society as we know it. I know some people worry about the potential risks, and I agree with them that we need to have an informed and cautious approach, but I think that the overall impact will still be overwhelmingly positive. General AI could help overcome one of the main limitations of humans: our limited memory and processing capacity. AI itself can assist with scientific research and maybe even independently invent things itself! 

What is your biggest strength?

Strength: I'd have to say that my biggest strength is my adaptability and determination. No matter what obstacles come in my way of something, I always figure out a way to overcome it. I think that this skill primarily results from my experience in electrical engineering, as there's always so many unexpected problems that come up.
